Why do you need a qualification?
By Amadou Saidy

Qualifications provide a standardized way to validate that an individual has acquired a certain level of knowledge and skills in a specific area. This is important in professions where competence and expertise are crucial. Many employers use qualifications as a screening tool during the hiring process. Having relevant qualifications can enhance your employability by signaling to employers that you possess the necessary skills and knowledge for the job. Qualifications often play a key role in career progression. They can open doors to higher-level positions and opportunities for advancement within a particular field.

Qualifications often have international recognition, making it easier for individuals to work or study in different countries. This is particularly true for degrees and certifications from well-established institutions.

While qualifications are valuable in many contexts, it's important to note that they are not the sole determinant of success or capability. Other factors, such as experience, practical skills, and personal qualities, also contribute to an individual's overall profile. Additionally, the importance of qualifications can vary across different industries and professions.
